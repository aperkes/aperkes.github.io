2020:

Honestly, I can't complain

2020:

  • December 31, 2020
  • Posted By Ammon Perkes
  • Misc

“That Ammon Perkes is really coming along!” ~Damian Woetzel, president of Julliard and artistic director of the Vail Dance Festival

2020 has been a pretty strange year for me, and it’s hard to remember what all has happened. I’ve spent the vast majority of the year working on one paper that has sometimes felt like a bottomless pit of work and struggle.

In the midst of all this, the compounding crises have just been minor distractions. I don’t mean to undercut the very real danger and hardship people have faced, but Chloe and I have been healthy, our jobs have continued, albeit over zoom. Our home easily accommodates the two of us, and we’re just the right mix of privilege to avoid the worst of almost everything.

So with that in mind, it’s been sort of frustrating for me that I haven’t been able to be more productive than I have. Why was it so hard for me to work, just because I was forced to do it from my desk instead of my office? I went into this year planning to complete my PhD, and I’m wrapping up December exactly where I was last year: accepting that my goal of submitting this paper by the end of the year isn’t going to happen.

There have been some highlights, and I will focus on them, to be a little more upbeat.

  1. I started feeling like a scientist. Toward the end of 2019 I was feeling pretty bitter about where things were, and I expressed a lot of that to my committee, and they basically told me to do my job and stop expecting my PI to do it for me. Maybe I’ll talk more about this idea later, but that really helped me to realize that I was responsible to ensure my project was interesting and sound, and the last year I’ve spent a lot of time transforming this paper from basically a bunch of crap that I hoped would show off how clever I was, to some scholarship that I hope will actually inform the way people think about animal behavior and evolution (and show off how clever I am. I still need to get a job.) So that’s been good, I’m still not perfect in that, but it’s been a big change. Related to that, for the first time I presented some results at a conference that I was both confident and excited about and I got some good feedback. I’ve talked about this a bit on here before, but it really is so much better to do science you’re passionate about.
  2. I made a website! This is still new for me, but it feels nice to be searchable.
  3. We published a paper! This is not the massive first author paper that I’m hinging my thesis on, but I got to do some very cool work with a good friend, Luke Anderson. You can find it here: open access through February. I’ll probably do a proper blog post all about it. This was a blast to work on, and I’m really proud of the work we did. We also published our first work on the smart aviary, which has been 5 years in the making. It’s awesome to have it out there, and there should be a lot more coming soon.
  4. I started rock climbing outside. In the past, I climbed outside a lot, but not since moving to Philadelphia. This actually feels a bit similar to taking responsibility for my own science. Maybe I’ll make a combined post about the two. In the meantime, I’ll just say the Philly has some really fun bouldering, and it’s been a lifesaver for me (as have the crash pads Chloe bought me)
  5. I made my dance debut! This was sort of silly, but it was a fun time. You can read about and watch it here. And now I can say I’ve gotten a good review by Damian Woetzel, president of Julliard and artistic director of the Vail Dance Festival.
  6. Zoom D&D: Maybe I’ll do a post about Dungeons and Dragons sometime. I’m always wary of getting too into my hobbies, for fear some future employer will think “No one who enjoys his hobbies so much will be tenure track material.” But that’s been a really wonderful new part of my life.
  7. We’re going to have a baby! This should probably be a post in its own right, but Chloe and I decided we were ready to take a crack at being parents. We’re both very excited. Interestingly, it takes much less time and work to create a human life than to make a decent paper. Stay tuned for updates on both, circa March, 2021.

So all in all, 2020 has been an intense and very challenging year. But we have mercifully been spared the worst of it, and it ended up being a year of some personal, professional, and fetal growth, and I think we will look back fondly on 2020.

I know 2020 has gone a lot worse for a lot of people, there’s nothing fair about how comfortable this year has been for us while other people have had their lives completely devastated. I’m not sure what to do about that other than be grateful and try to make life less terrible for other people.

Here’s to a new year of making life less terrible. And maybe figuring out blog formatting a bit better. Honestly this thing is a mess.

~ap
